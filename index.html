Our Dataset

Gun Violence Archive

All of our data originates from the Gun Violence Archive, a not for profit corporation formed in 2013 to provide free online public access to accurate information about gun-related violence in the United States. They are an independent data collection and research group with no affiliation to any advocacy organization.

Their information is collected from over 2,500 media, law enforcement, government and commercial sources daily in an effort to provide near-real time data about the results of gun violence.

James Ko's Kaggle Data

In April of 2018, James Ko released a public dataset on Kaggle scraped off of the Gun Violence Archives website in order to see what various data scientists could do with a more complete resource than what had been readily available up to that point.

As of March 2019, 76 people on Kaggle have worked with the data James provided and hosted their final kernels on the site. That data set has also been used in many other projects that you can find online, including this article on the Mapbox blog by Mikel Maron.

What We've Done

Once we had a hold of James Ko's data from the Gun Violence Archive, we then cleaned up the data as it existed. Adding in the missing incidents that James was unable to originally scrape and using Geocoder to input any missing latitude and longitude were primary among our efforts.

With the data cleaned up, we parsed through it to make it more compatible with the various visualizations that we deliver to our end users, fully maintaining the integrity of the original data as it was collected by the Gun Violence Archive.
